About Indira Gandhi Indira Gandhi was an Indian politician and the first female Prime Minister of India. She was born on November 19, 1917, in Allahabad, British India, and served as Prime Minister from 1966 to 1977 and again from 1980 until her assassination in 1984. Indira Gandhi played a key role in shaping the modern political landscape of India. She implemented various policies and initiatives to promote social justice, economic development, and national security. During her tenure, she focused on the welfare of the poor and marginalized sections of society, introduced land reforms, and advocated for the empowerment of women.

  • Indira Gandhi, the first female Prime Minister of India, was a prominent and influential figure in Indian politics. Born on November 19, 1917, in Allahabad, she inherited her political acumen from her father, Jawaharlal Nehru, who was India's first Prime Minister. Indira Gandhi's political career began after her father's death in 1964, and she quickly rose through the ranks of the Indian National Congress party.   • However, her tenure was not without controversy. In 1975, Indira Gandhi declared a state of emergency, suspending civil liberties and imposing strict censorship. This period was marked by allegations of human rights abuses and political repression. The emergency lasted for 21 months before being lifted in 1977.

  • Tragically, Indira Gandhi's political journey came to an abrupt end when she was assassinated on October 31, 1984, by her own bodyguards at her residence in New Delhi. Her assassination led to widespread political turmoil and violence across the country. Despite the controversies and challenges she faced, Indira Gandhi's legacy continues to be significant in Indian politics. She was a trailblazing female leader who shattered barriers, serving as an inspiration for women in India and around the world. Her contributions to India's development, her advocacy for social justice, and her influential role in shaping the country's foreign policy make her an enduring figure in Indian history.
